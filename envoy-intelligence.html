The Platform

A new kind of process intelligence.

Envoy Intelligence combines three capabilities that individually exist in the industry — real-time monitoring, predictive modeling, and expert knowledge systems — into a single, engineer-supervised platform that operates continuously and responds in real time.

Most process analytics tools do one thing: they show you data, or they flag anomalies, or they run models. Envoy Intelligence connects all three layers. When a predictive model detects an early failure signature, the system cross-references the pattern against Envoy's knowledge base of real operational events, surfaces the most probable causes, and delivers a structured response recommendation — directly to the operator who needs to act on it.

The result is something that doesn't exist elsewhere in pulp and paper: a system that knows your process, learns from every event, and responds with the institutional knowledge of two decades of mill engineering — in real time.

Real-Time Alerts

The Envoy Advisor — your continuous process sentinel.

The Envoy Advisor is the real-time interface through which Envoy Intelligence communicates with your mill team. Unlike a dashboard that requires someone to be watching, the Advisor pushes actionable alerts when conditions warrant attention — with context, not just data.

What Triggers an Alert

Alerts are triggered by the predictive models and rules engine working in concert — not by simple threshold crossings. A single variable moving out of range may not trigger an alert; a pattern of three variables shifting together in a historically meaningful way will. This dramatically reduces alert fatigue while ensuring nothing important is missed.

What Comes With Each Alert

Every Advisor alert includes the triggering condition, the process context (what else changed in the preceding minutes and hours), the most probable causes ranked by likelihood, and the recommended actions drawn from Envoy's Failure-Cause-Fix knowledge base. Operators get structured guidance, not raw data.

How Alerts Are Delivered

Alerts are delivered via the channels your team actually uses — email, SMS, or integration with your existing control room display systems. There's no new application to learn and no dashboard to keep open. The Advisor comes to your team, not the other way around.

Continuous Learning

Every alert that's resolved — whether the recommended action was taken and whether it worked — feeds back into the system. Over time, the Advisor becomes better calibrated to your specific process, your specific equipment, and the actual failure modes your mill experiences.

The Technology

Physics-based machine learning — and why it matters.

Most machine learning models in industrial applications are purely data-driven: given enough historical examples, they learn to recognize patterns. This approach has real limitations in process industries, where operating regimes shift, data is often sparse in the conditions that matter most, and a model that can't explain its predictions can't be trusted by an engineer.

Envoy Intelligence uses physics-based machine learning — also called hybrid or physics-informed modeling — which embeds known process relationships as constraints within the learning framework. The model "knows," from first principles, that steam pressure cannot exceed boiler design limits, that consistency affects formation in predictable ways, and that certain chemical reactions have known kinetics. It doesn't have to learn these relationships from data; it starts with them.

The practical consequences are significant:

  • More reliable predictions in sparse-data regimes — the regions that matter most (failure conditions, extreme grades) often have the least historical data. Physics fills the gap.
  • Fewer false positives — the model can't predict physically impossible outcomes, which eliminates a significant class of spurious alerts.
  • Interpretable outputs — when a prediction can be traced back to a physical mechanism, engineers can validate it, trust it, and explain it to operations leadership.
  • Better generalization — a physics-constrained model adapts more reliably when raw materials, grades, or equipment change than a purely data-driven model trained on historical conditions.

The Knowledge Base

Failure-Cause-Fix: two decades of mill events, codified.

When a problem occurs at a mill, the event itself is rarely what costs money. It's the time between the problem starting and the right person knowing what to do about it. Failure-Cause-Fix is the system Envoy has built to compress that time to seconds.

How it works

Over 20+ years of working inside pulp and paper mills, Envoy's engineers have accumulated a structured library of process failure patterns — the conditions that precede them, the causes that drive them, and the corrective actions that resolve them. This library is organized as a searchable, matchable knowledge base.

When Envoy Intelligence detects a pattern that resembles a known failure signature, it queries the Failure-Cause-Fix library and returns the most probable causes (ranked by likelihood given the current process state) alongside the recommended actions that have resolved similar situations in the past.

The result: when a break happens, or a quality excursion begins, or an equipment anomaly develops, the operator doesn't have to rely on memory or experience alone. They have immediate access to the collective judgment of Envoy's entire engineering team — distilled into structured guidance, available in seconds.

What it covers

Paper Machine Breaks

Pre-break signatures, probable initiating causes, corrective steps for each cause category, and preventive conditions to monitor to reduce recurrence.

Quality Excursions

Conditions that correlate with basis weight, caliper, moisture, and strength deviations — with the process variables most likely to be driving them.

Equipment Anomalies

Pump, fan, valve, and drive failure signatures — with recommended inspection or corrective actions at each stage of degradation.

Chemical & Energy Deviations

Unexpected consumption patterns for steam, power, bleach chemicals, and water — with likely process causes and corrective guidance.
