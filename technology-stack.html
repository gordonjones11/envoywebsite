The Intelligent Factory:   The Technology Stack

The technology stack behind the intelligent mill.

From sensors and historians to analytics and decision support — the intelligent factory is built in layers. Understanding those layers is the first step to building them well.

← Intelligent Factory: Start Here

The Stack

Six layers, one direction of flow.

Value flows upward — from the equipment you already own to the improvements that show up on the P&L. Each layer builds on the one below it, and skipping a layer is where most transformations stall.

1

Physical Equipment

Digesters, paper machines, boilers, pumps, and valves — the mill you already own. The intelligent factory starts with the assets in place today, not the ones in a capital plan.

2

Industrial Data

Sensors, historians, quality systems, and maintenance records capturing what's happening — thousands of tags, most of them already collected and already paid for.

3

Operational Context

The layer most mills are missing. Asset hierarchy, operating states, grades, and process events give raw data meaning — turning a wall of tags into questions that can actually be answered.

4

Analytics & AI

Statistics, process models, and machine learning applied to contextualized data. With context underneath it, analytics finds causes — without it, it finds correlations.

5

Decision Support

Insights delivered into the workflow — morning reports, real-time alerts, operator copilots. Intelligence only matters when it reaches the person making the decision, in time to act.

6

Operational Improvement

The point of the whole stack: throughput, yield, reliability, energy, and profit per hour. If a layer doesn't ultimately move these numbers, it's decoration.
