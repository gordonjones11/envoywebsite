Areas of Focus

What our engineers look for — every night.

Our engineers aren't running the same canned report for every mill. They know your process, your equipment history, and your current targets. Here are the categories of issues they investigate nightly across your operation.

Pre-Break Signatures

Process conditions that historically precede sheet breaks — tension anomalies, moisture swings, basis weight instability — flagged before they cascade into downtime.

Energy & Chemical Deviations

Unexpected consumption patterns in steam, power, bleach chemicals, and water — often the earliest signal of equipment performance degradation or process drift.

Control Loop Performance

PIDs in saturation, loops hunting, valves at limits — degraded control loop health is a silent driver of instability that many mills don't monitor systematically.

Off-Quality & Grade Risk

Variables trending toward specification limits, consistency issues, or conditions that have historically correlated with customer complaints or out-of-spec rolls.

Equipment Health Signals

Flow, pressure, and temperature deviations that suggest bearing wear, pump cavitation, fouling, or other maintenance-relevant conditions — before failure occurs.

Process Stability Trends

Multi-day trends showing gradual drift in key variables — the kind of slow-moving shift that's invisible day-to-day but significant over a week or a month.

CUSUM Control Charts

Cumulative Sum charts detect subtle, sustained shifts in a process mean that standard control charts miss entirely. Where a Shewhart chart flags large, sudden excursions, CUSUM accumulates small deviations over time — making it highly sensitive to the gradual drift that typically precedes process problems in mill environments.

Time Series Analysis

Identifying trends, cycles, and anomalies across hundreds of process variables simultaneously. Our models account for the natural operating patterns of your mill — shift changes, grade changes, seasonal effects — so what we flag is genuinely unusual, not just normal variation.

Quartile & Distribution Analysis

Understanding how a variable performs across the full range of operating conditions — not just its average. Quartile analysis reveals whether a process is performing in the best quarter of its historical range, or has quietly slipped to the bottom — a distinction that averages hide completely.

Going Deeper

When you need more than the daily report.

The daily report covers what changed overnight. Comprehensive reports go deeper — answering the harder questions about why something happened, what's connected to what, and where your process is losing ground. These are available on request and scheduled on a regular basis for clients who want them.

Correlation Studies

Finding hidden relationships between process variables that aren't obvious from daily observation. Often reveals that a quality problem has a root cause several steps upstream — or that two variables that seem unrelated are actually driving each other. These studies have changed how many of our clients think about their process.

Root Cause Analysis

When something goes wrong — a break, an off-quality run, an unexpected energy spike — our engineers trace it backward through process data to identify the initiating cause. This goes beyond the event log to examine what conditions existed in the hours and days before the problem occurred.

Time Period Comparisons

Comparing mill performance before and after a change — a new grade, a maintenance event, a chemical reformulation, a seasonal transition. Quantifies whether the change actually helped, and if so, where, and by how much. The discipline of measurement your team rarely has time to do itself.

Control Loop Health

A systematic audit of your PID control loops: which are in saturation, which are hunting, which have valves at their limits, and which are simply not contributing to process stability. Degraded control loops are one of the most common — and most overlooked — contributors to process instability in mature mill environments.

Getting Started

Most clients are live within a week.

There's no software to install, no lengthy onboarding, and no learning curve for your team. Here's how it works.

1

Connect your data

We work with your IT team to establish a read-only connection to your process historian — OSIsoft PI, Aspentech IP.21, or similar. This is typically the only technical lift required, and we handle all the configuration on our end.

2

Your engineer gets up to speed

Your dedicated Envoy engineer reviews your process setup, equipment history, and current operating targets. We identify which process areas to prioritize and configure the initial exception thresholds for your operation.

3

Reports start arriving

Your daily reports begin. For the first few weeks, your Envoy engineer is in close contact — calibrating findings to your feedback, refining what's relevant, and getting attuned to your mill's patterns. Most clients find real value in the first week.
