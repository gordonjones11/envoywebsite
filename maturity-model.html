The Intelligent Factory:   The Maturity Model

Where is your mill on the intelligence curve?

Reactive → Connected → Contextualized → Intelligent → Adaptive. Every mill sits somewhere on this five-level curve — and knowing where you are matters more than knowing where you're headed.

← Intelligent Factory: Start Here

The Model

Five levels of operational intelligence.

Maturity isn't about how much technology a mill owns — it's about how decisions get made. Each level marks a real shift in how the operation detects, decides, and learns.

1

Reactive Operations

Problems are discovered after the fact. Knowledge lives in a few experienced heads, shifts firefight independently, and yesterday's issues repeat next month.

2

Connected Operations

Data is captured and accessible — historians running, tags trending. But it's a wall of numbers: the mill is data-rich and answer-poor.

3

Contextualized Operations

Data is organized around assets, grades, operating states, and events. Questions like "how does this grade run on this machine?" finally have fast, reliable answers.

4

Intelligent Operations

Analytics and AI surface issues before they become failures, and decision support reaches operators and engineers inside their daily workflow — not in a dashboard nobody opens.

5

Adaptive Operations

The mill continuously learns. Every upset becomes knowledge, every shift builds on the last, and improvement compounds — with people still firmly in charge.
